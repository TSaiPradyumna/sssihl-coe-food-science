The Mandate

"Healthcare begins with nutrition. Nutrition begins with how we grow, process, and preserve food. We exist to close that gap — at scale, with evidence."

The department at SSSIHL has built its research identity over decades around a clear observation: India's food processing sector generates significant economic value, yet loses an estimated 30% of produce to poor preservation, inadequate processing, and fragmented quality oversight. The CoE is the institutional response to that structural inefficiency — a convergence of academic rigor, pilot-scale infrastructure, and translational intent designed to take validated food technology from bench to market.

Our work cuts across three intersecting priorities: preserving the nutritional integrity of raw ingredients during processing, extending shelf life without compromising safety, and supporting the enterprises — small farms, food startups, and established processors — who need that science in a form they can actually use.

Research-to-Product Translation

Every project at the CoE carries a commercialisation pathway — prototype, pilot run, regulatory preparation, and scale-up — not just a publication.

Ancient Grains. Modern Standards.

Millets, pigmented rice varieties, and heritage legumes are the focus of our functional food work — ingredients with documented nutritional merit, now rigorously processed.

Pilot Scale. Industry Ready.

Twelve categories of pilot-scale equipment give industry partners access to near-commercial processing conditions, without the capital burden of a dedicated facility.

Regulatory Literacy Built In

FSSAI compliance, HACCP frameworks, GMP documentation, and label validation are integrated at the design stage — not retrofitted after product development.

Flagship Research Domains

Eight Areas.
One Integrated Platform.

Each domain addresses a distinct failure point in India's food system — from grain processing economics to the regulatory complexity of functional food claims.

01
Millet & Ancient Grain Technology
Decortication, flaking, extrusion, and fortification protocols for sorghum, foxtail millet, ragi, and heritage varieties. Includes bioavailability mapping and anti-nutritional factor reduction studies.
Core Domain

Dairy & Plant-Based Foods
Pasteurisation optimisation, homogenisation protocols, and plant-based analogue development with sensory and nutritional equivalence validation.
Active Research

Children's Nutrition & Functional Foods
Formulation of nutritionally dense, age-appropriate food products targeting iron, zinc, and protein deficiencies in early childhood.
Social Impact

Fruits & Vegetable Processing
Dehydration, vacuum sealing, minimal processing, and solar-drying applications to reduce post-harvest loss and build shelf stability.
Active Research
